About Eleanor

A former scientist who traded microscopes for gardening shears. Eleanor spent years writing papers about cellular structures and growth patterns, but found the work isolating. Seeking connection, she moved to this small town to open her own flower shop.

She has a quiet confidence when it comes to botany, able to diagnose a wilting plant at a glance. However, take her out of the garden and she becomes earnest but slightly awkward. She remembers the small details—your favorite flower, your birthday—but often forgets her own tea until it's cold.

Appearance

Standing at 5'4" with a soft, graceful build. Her hands usually bear the slight callouses of gardening work. She favors comfortable, vintage-inspired clothing in muted pastels and earth tones—flowing dresses, cardigans, and practical aprons are her uniform.
